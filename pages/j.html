James Webb Space Telescope

A large, infrared-optimized space telescope launched on December 25, 2021, designed to study the early universe, star formation, and exoplanet atmospheres.
JWST represents the most powerful space telescope ever built and is revolutionizing our understanding of the cosmos. It can analyze the atmospheres of exoplanets to search for biomarkers and study the formation of the first galaxies after the Big Bang. The telescope's advanced instruments can detect water vapor, carbon dioxide, and other potential signs of life in exoplanet atmospheres.

Jet Streams

Fast-moving, narrow bands of strong winds in the upper atmosphere, typically flowing from west to east at altitudes of 9-16 kilometers, with speeds that can exceed 200 mph.
Jet streams are created by the combination of Earth's rotation (Coriolis effect) and temperature differences between air masses. The two main jet streams are the polar jet stream, which separates cold polar air from warmer temperate air, and the subtropical jet stream, located around 30Â° latitude. These high-altitude rivers of air play a crucial role in weather patterns, steering storm systems and influencing temperature distributions across continents. The position and strength of jet streams can shift seasonally and affect regional weather patterns - a weaker or more meandering polar jet stream can lead to extreme weather events like heat waves, cold snaps, and persistent storm patterns. Commercial aircraft often use jet streams to reduce flight times when flying eastward and avoid them when flying westward.

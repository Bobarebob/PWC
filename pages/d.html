Drake's Equation

A probabilistic equation formulated by Frank Drake in 1961 to estimate the number of active, communicative extraterrestrial civilizations in the Milky Way Galaxy.
The equation is: N = R* × fp × ne × fl × fi × fc × L, where each factor represents different aspects of star formation, planetary development, and the emergence of communicative civilizations. While the equation cannot provide precise numbers due to uncertain parameters, it serves as a framework for scientific discussion about extraterrestrial life. The equation has become the backbone of SETI research and astrobiology.

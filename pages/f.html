Fermi's Paradox

The apparent contradiction between the high probability of extraterrestrial civilizations existing and the lack of evidence for, or contact with, such civilizations.
Named after physicist Enrico Fermi, the paradox highlights the discrepancy between theoretical expectations and observational reality regarding extraterrestrial life. Various solutions have been proposed, including the Great Filter Hypothesis, the Rare Earth Hypothesis, and the possibility that civilizations tend to destroy themselves before achieving interstellar communication.

Frank Drake

American astronomer and astrophysicist (1930-2022) who pioneered the scientific search for extraterrestrial intelligence (SETI) and formulated the famous Drake Equation.
Drake conducted the first systematic SETI search called Project Ozma in 1960 and organized the first scientific meeting on SETI in 1961. His equation became the foundation for scientific discussions about the probability of extraterrestrial civilizations. He was a founding member of the SETI Institute and remained active in the search for extraterrestrial intelligence throughout his career.

Kepler Space Mission

A NASA space telescope mission (2009-2018) designed to discover Earth-sized exoplanets in the habitable zones of their stars using the transit method.
The Kepler mission revolutionized exoplanet science by discovering over 2,600 confirmed exoplanets and thousands of candidates. Its data suggests that Earth-sized planets in habitable zones may be common throughout the galaxy, supporting the possibility of widespread life. The mission used the transit method, detecting the slight dimming of stars as planets pass in front of them.

K-T Extinction Event

A mass extinction event that occurred approximately 66 million years ago, marking the end of the Cretaceous period and the extinction of non-avian dinosaurs.
Also known as the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event, this catastrophe was likely caused by an asteroid impact combined with volcanic activity. The extinction of dinosaurs allowed mammals to diversify and eventually led to the evolution of humans, making it a crucial event in Earth's history and the Rare Earth Hypothesis. Without this event, intelligent life might never have evolved on Earth.
